NYC GUIDE FOR NEWCOMERS

Moving to NYC is a dream for lots of people. But it can become a nightmare if you don’t prepare your move properly. Have in mind that living in NYC is not cheap at all and you should filter all your belongings. Think of what you haven’t been using in the past year and get rid of these items. Or, you can store them for a while. In that way, you can save on not transporting unnecessary stuff. After sorting your items, prepare yourself for packing and moving. Blend in

Learn slang. This is a good way to blend in with your new environment. As a New Yorker, you should know that ”The Island” refers to Long Island and not Manhattan or Staten Island. “Whip” is an expression for an expensive car, and “guap” is an expression used for money. Also, pizza will become your new favorite meal because $1 Pizza is something you will find pretty much anywhere.

NYC Guide for newcomers to keep handy

One thing is for sure, NYC is not the place where you will be bored. It is a city that never sleeps. These are several things that NYC has to offer:

  • Skyline views. The most popular is the Empire State Building. It is the most romantic attraction in town. It has played a role in many romantic films. Besides this, there is also Top of the Rock, One World Observatory, and the Roof of the Metropolitan Museum.

  • Visit Central Park. Whether you want to go for a walk, drive a bike or run, this is a perfect place.

  • Watch a Broadway show. This is a must. It is a bit pricey but it is worth it.

  • Visit Times Square at Night. It is one of the most iconic places in the world. It is worth a visit at any time of day, but especially at night.

  • Social life. The last thing in this NYC guide for newcomers but also important is not to forget about your social life. NYC has a lot to offer. Bars, clubs, pubs, expensive restaurants, and fast-food restaurants.

This is a short NYC guide for newcomers. Hopefully, these tips will make your new start in NYC easier.
